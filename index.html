Fig: Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal

Type: Monument

Location: Dharmapuri, Forest Colony, Tajganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India - 282001

Construction Started: 1632 AD

Construction Completed: 1653 AD

Why was it Built?
Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan built it in the memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Dimensions: 73m in height, 17 hectares in area

Materials Used:
Semi-precious stones such as Agate, Turquoise, Lapis Lazuli, Coral, Onyx, Cat’s Eye, Bloodstone, and translucent white marble.

Architectural Style: Mughal Architecture

Designer: Ustad Ahmad Lahauri, Ustad Isa

Visit Timing:

  • Daytime: Sunrise to sunset every day (06:00 AM to 06:30 PM) except Friday.
  • Friday: Taj Mahal is closed and only accessible in the afternoon for Muslims to attend prayers.
  • Nighttime: 08:30 PM to 12:30 AM
  • Night View: Only available 5 days a month - full moon night, 2 days before, and 2 days after (except Fridays and during Ramadan).

Entry Fee:

  • Foreigners: 1100 INR
  • Indians: 50 INR
  • Citizens of SAARC or BIMSTEC countries: 540 INR
  • Children below 15: Free

How to Reach:

From NCR, you can drive down the 165-kilometer-long Yamuna Expressway connecting Greater Noida with Agra. Agra is 235 kilometers from Jaipur and 125 kilometers from Gwalior.

Description:

The Taj Mahal is one of the world's wonders and is also among the UNESCO World Heritage Sites! The Taj Mahal has significance far beyond just being a monument. It deserves your visit at least once. With fountains leading to the mausoleum, crafted in soft & pure marble and adorned with semi-precious stones, it is a symbol of love where the Queen rests in peace with her King. Beauty lies in the eyes of the beholder, and the Taj Mahal exemplifies this perfectly. It displays different moods through its varied shades, making it a masterpiece of architectural beauty. Visiting the Taj Mahal will leave you mesmerized, making you feel the presence of Shah Jahan, the visionary behind it, and Mumtaz Mahal, for whom it was built. Come to the Taj Mahal and experience its grandeur—fall in love with it every time!


Fig: India Gate

India Gate

Type: War Memorial

Location: Rajpath, New Delhi, India

Construction Started: 1921

Inauguration: 12 February 1931

Why was it Built?
India Gate was built as a war memorial to honor the 84,000 soldiers of the British Indian Army who lost their lives during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

Dimensions: 42m in height

Materials Used:
Yellow and red sandstone sourced from Bharatpur, Rajasthan.

Architectural Style: Triumphal Arch

Designer: Sir Edwin Lutyens

Visit Timing:

  • Open: 24 hours
  • Best Time to Visit: Evening (to see the beautifully illuminated structure)

Entry Fee:

  • Entry: Free for all visitors

How to Reach:

India Gate is centrally located in New Delhi and is easily accessible by road. The nearest metro stations are Central Secretariat (Yellow Line) and Barakhamba Road (Blue Line). Public transport, including buses, auto-rickshaws, and taxis, is available.

Description:

India Gate is one of the most iconic war memorials in India, standing proudly in the heart of Delhi. It was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and inspired by the Arc de Triomphe in Paris. The monument bears the names of over 13,000 soldiers who sacrificed their lives. In 1972, the Amar Jawan Jyoti (Flame of the Immortal Soldier) was added to honor the martyrs of the Indo-Pakistan War of 1971. India Gate is a popular tourist attraction, especially in the evenings when it is beautifully lit. The lush green lawns surrounding the monument serve as a perfect spot for visitors to relax and admire the structure. Standing as a tribute to the bravery of Indian soldiers, India Gate is more than just a memorial—it is a symbol of national pride and resilience.


Fig: Statue of Unity

Statue of Unity

Type: Monument

Location: Kevadia, Narmada District, Gujarat, India

Construction Started: 31 October 2013

Inauguration: 31 October 2018

Why was it Built?
The Statue of Unity was built to honor Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, the Iron Man of India, who played a key role in the unification of the country.

Dimensions: 182m in height, making it the world's tallest statue

Materials Used:
Bronze cladding, reinforced concrete, steel framework, and structural steel.

Architectural Style: Modern Monumental Architecture

Designer: Ram V. Sutar

Visit Timing:

  • Open: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Best Time to Visit: Winter season (October to March) for pleasant weather

Entry Fee:

  • Adults: 150 INR (Viewing gallery ticket - 380 INR)
  • Children (3-15 years): 90 INR (Viewing gallery ticket - 230 INR)
  • Children below 3 years: Free

How to Reach:

The Statue of Unity is located near Kevadia in Gujarat. The nearest airport is Vadodara (90 km), and the nearest railway station is Kevadia Railway Station. It is well connected by road from major cities like Ahmedabad (200 km) and Surat (154 km).

Description:

The Statue of Unity is the tallest statue in the world, standing at an incredible height of 182 meters. It was built as a tribute to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, India's first Deputy Prime Minister and a key leader in the country’s independence movement. The statue stands on the banks of the Narmada River, facing the Sardar Sarovar Dam, symbolizing unity, strength, and progress. The site includes a museum, exhibition hall, and an observation deck at 153 meters, offering a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. The Statue of Unity represents India's gratitude to Sardar Patel for his immense contribution in uniting 562 princely states after independence, making India a single, sovereign nation. Visiting this engineering marvel is a once-in-a-lifetime experience. Witness the grandeur of this monument and get inspired by the legacy of Sardar Patel.


Fig: Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar

Type: Monument

Location: Mehrauli, New Delhi, India

Construction Started: 1199 AD

Construction Completed: 1220 AD

Why was it Built?
The Qutub Minar was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak to celebrate Muslim dominance in Delhi after defeating the last Hindu kingdom.

Dimensions: 72.5m in height, with a diameter of 14.3m at the base and 2.7m at the top

Materials Used:
Red sandstone and marble with intricate carvings and inscriptions from the Quran.

Architectural Style: Indo-Islamic Architecture

Designer: Qutb-ud-din Aibak (started), Iltutmish and Firoz Shah Tughlaq (completed)

Visit Timing:

  • Open: 7:00 AM to 10:00 PM (All days of the week)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather

Entry Fee:

  • Foreigners: 600 INR
  • Indians: 40 INR
  • Children below 15: Free

How to Reach:

The Qutub Minar is well connected by road and metro. The nearest metro station is Qutub Minar Metro Station on the Yellow Line, just 1.5 km away. It is about 15 km from Connaught Place and 12 km from India Gate.

Description:

The Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It stands as a symbol of Delhi’s rich historical heritage. The tower consists of five distinct storeys, adorned with bands of inscriptions and intricate carvings. It is believed to be inspired by the Minaret of Jam in Afghanistan. Surrounding the Qutub Minar complex are significant structures like the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, Iron Pillar of Delhi, and Alauddin’s Madrasa, making it an important site for history enthusiasts. Legends say that the Iron Pillar, standing nearby, has remained rust-free for over 1600 years, showcasing ancient Indian metallurgy skills. Visiting the Qutub Minar is an awe-inspiring experience, as it takes you back in time to the early days of the Delhi Sultanate. It’s a must-visit monument for history lovers and travelers alike. Come and witness the grandeur of this architectural marvel and immerse yourself in Delhi’s glorious past!


Fig: Gateway of India

Gateway of India

Type: Monument

Location: Apollo Bandar, Colaba, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India - 400001

Construction Started: 1913 AD

Construction Completed: 1924 AD

Why was it Built?
The Gateway of India was built to commemorate the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary in India in 1911.

Dimensions: 26m (85 feet) in height

Materials Used:
Yellow basalt and reinforced concrete

Architectural Style: Indo-Saracenic with influences of Roman and Mughal architecture

Designer: George Wittet

Visit Timing:

  • Open: 24 hours a day
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather

Entry Fee:

  • Entry: Free for all visitors
  • Ferry Rides: Starting from 100 INR per person

How to Reach:

The Gateway of India is well connected by road and local transport. The nearest railway station is Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT), around 2.5 km away. It is also accessible via the Mumbai local train and bus networks.

Description:

The Gateway of India is one of Mumbai’s most iconic landmarks and a must-visit for history enthusiasts and tourists alike. Standing majestically on the shores of the Arabian Sea, it was originally built to welcome British royalty and later became a departure point for British troops when India gained independence in 1947. The monument’s grand structure, inspired by Indo-Saracenic, Mughal, and Roman architectural styles, makes it a fascinating sight to behold. Surrounding the Gateway, you can explore Mumbai’s bustling Colaba district, enjoy a ferry ride to Elephanta Caves, or simply relax by the sea while admiring the city’s vibrant atmosphere. At night, the Gateway of India is beautifully illuminated, making it an enchanting sight and a perfect spot for photography. Visit the Gateway of India to witness a blend of history, culture, and scenic beauty—an experience you will cherish forever!


Fig: Meenakshi Amman Temple

Meenakshi Amman Temple

Type: Hindu Temple

Location: Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India - 625001

Construction Started: 6th Century AD (original structure)

Construction Completed: Expanded significantly in the 16th century under Nayak rulers

Why was it Built?
The temple is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (a form of Parvati) and Lord Sundareswarar (a form of Shiva). It symbolizes divine love and is a sacred site for Hindu devotees.

Dimensions: Spread over 14 acres with 14 towering gopurams (gateway towers)

Materials Used:
Granite, sandstone, and intricately carved sculptures depicting Hindu mythology.

Architectural Style: Dravidian Architecture

Designer: Ancient Tamil artisans, later expanded by the Nayak rulers

Visit Timing:

  • Morning: 05:00 AM to 12:30 PM
  • Evening: 04:00 PM to 10:00 PM
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather

Entry Fee:

  • Entry: Free for general darshan
  • Special Darshan: 100 INR to 500 INR (depending on the type of darshan)
  • Photography: 50 INR for mobile, 100 INR for cameras

How to Reach:

The temple is located in Madurai, which is well connected by air, rail, and road. The nearest airport is Madurai International Airport (12 km away). Madurai Junction Railway Station is just 1.5 km from the temple.

Description:

Meenakshi Amman Temple is one of the most magnificent Hindu temples in India, known for its vibrant gopurams, detailed carvings, and rich spiritual heritage. Dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar, the temple complex is a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture, featuring thousands of sculpted figures, colorful towers, and sacred ponds. The temple is the heart of Madurai, a city with over 2,500 years of history. It plays a major role in the Meenakshi Thirukalyanam festival, celebrated grandly every year. The temple is home to the Thousand Pillar Hall, a breathtaking corridor with intricately carved stone pillars that create musical sounds when tapped. Visitors to Meenakshi Temple can witness the daily rituals, temple processions, and evening Aarti, experiencing the deep spiritual energy of the place. Come and explore the divine aura of Meenakshi Amman Temple—a symbol of Tamil culture, devotion, and artistic excellence!
